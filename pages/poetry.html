A poem on the city of Fedora,
From Italo Calvino’s Invisible Cities.
2015

I shall tell you about Fedora next,
Nothing there was constant, valley or the crest.
I stood there, in a narrow lane,
At one point experiencing sun, snow and rain.
Illusional, I could call the city
Or delusional, myself.
Then I happened to meet the globes,
Alone in an empty room, through their lens.
The city veiled in mystery, started to make sense,
A finicky, aged prince, who altered,
Very frequently his long robes,
Every change being painted and framed.




Not a problem
Written for “No Acting Please”, an open-elective at the National Institute of Design.
2017

Everything that comes to you from now,
Is going to be something new any how
And that is life and it goes on,
You were ready for bed and it’s already dawn
It ain’t that bad to make you sad
Because it’s your choice, you should be glad
At every point we get to decide
What is to be left behind
Or kept by our side
So, not a problem, that’s all I’ll say
Now go on,
Theres so much to achieve every single day.




Kunda
Space, Form and Structure.
National Institute of Design
2017

Kunda grew a lettuce garden
The child could also cook
But to her aunt, the orphan was a burden
She imagined, but couldn’t look.

Every morning, the koyal coos.
Kunda awaits the sound of the heavy shoes
They sit, Kunda is thankful she can listen
To the man who talks about how is cows glisten
How His home is secluded on the hill
And how he works hard at the mill
He always has some milk to give
But Kunda wanted much more
She gave him lettuce but wanted a purpose to live
But the thought made her sad and sore.
He could never love her
They could never be together Some other solace she had to find,
Because at the end she would always remain blind.




Waters, here and there
National Institute of Design
2018.

Waters of the Sabarmati River may sooth my soul

But waves of the Arabian Sea will always be home.

There, the black, small gentle waves rushing towards the rocks

But here they are, amalgamation of different glitters, that light up the city.

There, the soft hearted rock pushes them away, proudly wearing the marks they leave

But here they stay still, oscillating amongst themselves, trying to find a home to return to.
